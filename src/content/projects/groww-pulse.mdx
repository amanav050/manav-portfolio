## The problem

Product teams drown in app store reviews. Reading 70+ a week to spot patterns is the kind of work that gets pushed off until it doesn't get done at all — and the signal is lost.

## What I built

A 4-step flow: CSV upload → AI theming → pulse generation → Gmail handoff. The output is a one-page weekly pulse covering 8 weeks of reviews — top themes, real user quotes, and three actionable ideas — generated in under 60 seconds. Stripe-inspired dark UI with a light/dark toggle, mobile responsive.

## Key decisions

I locked JSON contracts across two LLM calls to ensure reproducible output, and enforced a strict "top 3 by review_count" rule to avoid subjective ranking drift. The original architecture used Claude + Gmail MCP for fully automated drafts. Mid-build, I pivoted to Groq (Llama 3.3 70B) and a direct Gmail compose URL under cost constraints. Because I had locked identical response schemas across providers, the pivot was reversible in two files with zero frontend changes — the production stack can switch back the moment budget allows.
The product is also PII-free by construction, with a 250-word pulse cap and deterministic clustering.

## What I learned

Strict schemas are the difference between drift and trust when swapping LLM providers. The same discipline that made the pivot painless is what makes the output reproducible week to week.
